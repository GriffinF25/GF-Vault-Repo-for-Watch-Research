## Marketplace Fee Assumptions (Cached)

See [[marketplace-fees]] for most current fees. Cache your research here if verifying:

### eBay (Watch Sales - US)
- Final value fee: 12.9% + $0.30
- Payment processing (PayPal/managed): ~3%
- Shipping: Seller sets (typically $15–25)
- Returns: 30-day returns can reduce net if item returned

### Chrono24
- Buyer premium: 8% (paid by buyer, not deducted from seller price)
- Payment methods: WISE transfer (low fees), card (higher fees)
- Shipping: Included in price or negotiated

### eBay Express
- Faster shipping, higher fee (typically 16–18%)
- Good for premium items targeting quick sales

## Source Quality Notes

**Highest Confidence Sources:**
- Your own [[pricing-transactions]] (use as baseline)
- eBay "Sold" listings with visible price
- Chrono24 "Sold" listings (when price shown)

**Medium Confidence Sources:**
- Auction house results (reputable auction firms)
- Dealer price guides
- Market index services

**Lower Confidence (Verify Before Using):**
- Active asking prices on Chrono24 (not sold prices)
- Forum discussion asking prices
- Instagram dealer asking prices
- Social media asking prices

## How to Use Cached Research

1. **During pricing analysis:** Search this file for exact reference before running new searches
2. **Check freshness:** Note the research date; if >60 days old, consider refreshing for active market
3. **Use as baseline:** Start with cached findings, refine with any new 2–3 recent examples
4. **Save search time:** Avoid re-researching references you've already analyzed
5. **Build confidence:** Use cached evidence to validate new findings

## When to Refresh Research

- **Rule:** Refresh if >90 days old and you're pricing a similar watch
- **Exception:** Fast-moving commodities (popular Rolex references) refresh every 30–45 days
- **Rare watches:** Research can be 6–12 months old if market stable
- **Market change:** If you see evidence of major price shift, refresh immediately
