## eBay (United States)

### Final Value Fees
- **Watches category:** 12.9% + $0.30 per sale
- **Alternatives (if applicable):** 15% for some categories
- **Free insertions:** Up to 500 listings/month (watch sellers typically have plenty)

### Payment Processing
- **eBay Managed Payments (standard):** ~2.35% + $0.30 (PayPal integration)
- **Alternative payment methods:** May vary

### Shipping
- **Typical watch shipping:** $15–25 via USPS Priority Mail
- **Insurance:** +$3–8 recommended
- **Combined shipping:** $18–33 typical for domestic

### Returns & Issues
- **Buyer protection:** 30-day return window; restocking fees not applicable
- **Chargebacks:** Rare but possible; reserve for ~1–2% of sales
- **Issue resolution:** eBay typically favors buyer in disputes

### Net Proceeds Calculation
```
Gross sale price: $X
Less: Final value fee (12.9% + 0.30): -$X
Less: Payment processing (~2.35%): -$X
Less: Shipping cost: -$X
Less: Insurance: -$X
= Net proceeds
```

### Example
- Gross: $5,000
- Final value fee: -$645.30
- Payment processing: -$117.75
- Shipping: -$25
- Insurance: -$5
- **Net proceeds: $4,207 (15.9% total fees)**

## Chrono24

### Selling Fees
- **Premium seller:** 8% buyer premium (paid by buyer, **not deducted** from your price)
- **Standard seller:** May vary (typically 8–10%)
- **Shipping:** Negotiated seller-to-buyer or included in price

### Payment Methods
- **WISE (preferred):** ~1–2% transfer fee
- **Bank transfer:** Varies by country
- **Card payment:** ~2–3% if accepting cards

### Net Proceeds Calculation
```
Gross sale price: $X (buyer pays premium on top)
Less: Shipping cost: -$X (if included in price)
Less: Payment processing (WISE): -$X
= Net proceeds
```

### Example
- Gross sale price (before premium): $5,000
- Buyer's premium (8%, paid by buyer): +$400 (you don't see this, but it offsets demand competition)
- Shipping (if included): -$25
- Payment processing (1.5%): -$75
- **Net proceeds: $4,900 (assuming seller pays shipping)**

## Local/Private Sales

### Direct Sale (No Platform)
- **Fees:** None (buyer pays you directly)
- **Risk:** No buyer protection, authenticity disputes possible
- **Payment method:** Bank transfer, cash (security risk)
- **Shipping:** Negotiated; typically seller covers (~$25) or buyer pays

### Net Proceeds Calculation
```
Sale price: $X
Less: Shipping (if you pay): -$X
= Net proceeds
```

### Private Sales (Local)
- **No fees** if cash handoff
- **Negotiation range:** Typically 5–15% below eBay equivalent due to no platform protection

## Auction Houses

### Christie's, Sotheby's, Bonhams (Estimate for watches)
- **Buyer premium:** 20–25% (paid by buyer)
- **Seller's commission:** 10–15% of hammer price
- **Estimate/insurance fees:** $200–500+
- **Shipping/insurance:** 2–5% of value

### Example
- Estimated value: $10,000
- Hammer price: $12,000
- Seller's commission (12%): -$1,440
- Buyer premium (20%, paid by buyer): +$2,400 (not in your proceeds, but affects competitiveness)
- **Net proceeds to seller: $10,560 (after 12% commission)**
- **Not recommended for watches under $5,000** — fees too high

## Business Assumptions (GF Vault)

### Location & Tax
- **Seller Location:** Tennessee (TN) — No sales tax on watch purchases
- **Shipping:** IFS negotiated rates (confirm per-transaction cost)
- **Impact:** No tax liability simplifies deal math vs. CA/NY sellers

### Default Profit Target
- **Minimum acceptable net profit:** ~$300 per transaction
- **Target net profit:** $500+ per transaction when realistic
- **Target ROI:** 8–15% (15–30 days holding)

### Carrying Costs (When Applicable)
- **Short-term holds (< 30 days):** $0 (negligible)
- **Longer holds (30+ days):** Reserve $50–150 for storage/insurance/time value
- **Service/repair reserves:** $150–400 per watch (varies by reference, condition)

### Marketplace Selection Strategy
- **Default for good margins:** Chrono24 (higher gross prices, lower seller fees)
- **Default for liquidity:** eBay (faster sales, larger audience)
- **For premium condition:** Chrono24 (attracts serious collectors)
- **For quick liquidation:** eBay or private sale (faster turnover)

### Condition Definition for Pricing
| Grade | Description | Typical eBay Price Discount |
|-------|-------------|---------------------------|
| **Excellent** | Mint or like-new, minimal signs of wear | Baseline |
| **Very Good** | Light wear, well-maintained, no major issues | -5% to -8% |
| **Good** | Visible wear, polished/serviced, functional | -10% to -15% |
| **Fair** | Significant wear, may need service | -20% to -30% |

## Tax & Regulatory

### Sales Tax (Variable by US State)
- **Most states:** No sales tax on used goods (sold by private sellers)
- **Some states:** 1–3% sales tax may apply; verify jurisdiction
- **Assumption:** Ignore sales tax unless your volume reaches business threshold

### Reporting (1099 Income)
- **eBay threshold:** $20,000+ in sales or 200+ transactions triggers 1099-K
- **Tax treatment:** Hobby vs. business (above-threshold becomes taxable business)
- **Recommendation:** Consult tax advisor; track transactions for reporting

2. **Comparing platforms:** Check the comparison table for fastest cash/lowest fees
3. **Setting margins:** Apply the business assumptions to ensure $300+ minimum profit
